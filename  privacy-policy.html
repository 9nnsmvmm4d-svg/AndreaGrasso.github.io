Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Andrea Grasso may collect, use, and protect personal information when you visit this website or get in touch through it.

Who is responsible for this website

This website is operated by Andrea Grasso. If you have any questions about this policy or about how your data is handled, you can contact: hello@andreagrasso.co.uk.

What information may be collected

Depending on how you use the site, the following categories of information may be collected:

  • Your name and email address if you contact me directly.
  • Any information you include in messages you send.
  • Technical information such as browser type, device type, IP address, and pages viewed, where relevant analytics or hosting tools are used.

How your information may be used

Your information may be used to:

  • Respond to enquiries and messages.
  • Provide information about services or projects you have asked about.
  • Maintain, secure, and improve the website.
  • Understand general website usage where analytics tools are enabled.

Legal basis

Where UK GDPR applies, personal information is generally processed on the basis of legitimate interests, the need to respond to your enquiry, or your consent where consent is required. UK organisations that collect personal data must explain who they are, why they use personal data, and what rights people have. [web:169][web:175]

How long information is kept

Personal information is only kept for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose it was collected, including responding to enquiries, maintaining records, or meeting legal obligations.

Sharing information

Your information is not sold. It may be processed by trusted service providers used to run the website, email, hosting, analytics, or related technical services where relevant.

Your rights

Depending on your circumstances, you may have rights to request access to your personal information, ask for corrections, request deletion, object to certain uses, or ask for restrictions on processing. UK government guidance says organisations using personal information must tell people about these rights. [web:169]

Cookies and tracking

Information about cookies and similar technologies is provided separately in the Cookie Policy. ICO guidance says cookie information should be clearly accessible and not simply buried inside a privacy notice. [web:154][web:168]

Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes to the website, services, or legal requirements.
